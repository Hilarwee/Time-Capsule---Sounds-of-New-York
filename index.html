Sounds of New York

A time capsule of my first month in New York, captured in voice memos. It carries the city’s sound, both alive and sharp, foreign and overstimulating. To arrive in a new city is to be thrown into its pulse with all your senses overwhelmed. This capsule doesn’t show you the city, it lets you feel it, offering a look into how I've taken it all in. Maybe put some headphones in, close your eyes, and listen.

  1. whoshing train from East Village to Park Slope, taken in the morning of Feb 11th for an excursion to Interference Archive.
  2. clicks of the heater in my room after it's turned off.*
  3. rumbles of the laundromat.
  4. Lola serenading me in a bar.
  5. the never ending blare of sirens.
  6. all the elevators talk to you here.
  7. a saturday night in East Village.
  8. saxophone player on the Union Square subway platform.
  9. heatpress beeping like crazy in the textile lab.
  10. Bedford Av subway station on a Monday evening, on the way home from a runway show at the Gutter.

*this sound is much more subtle than the rest, please don't forget to turn the volume back down if you turned it up.
